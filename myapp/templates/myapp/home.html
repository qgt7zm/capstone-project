What is Gamification?

Rationale

Students today suffer from a lack of motivation and knowledge retention. Schools prioritize memorization and standardization, leaving developing children and adolescents unable to express their individuality and critical thinking. Meanwhile, video games have captured the minds of young learners, often for hours at a time.

So why not turn learning into to play? Gamification is the application of game elements in non-game contexts to improve productivity and achievement through fun. Some of its most cited benefits include increased motivation, engagement, and grades. Gamification helps bring the excitement back to school by giving students room to develop their creativity, teamwork, and problem solving through self-directed experimentation without fear of failure.

Say No to Hype

Chances are, you have noticed the rapid spread of gamified software in the past decade, from learning software such as Kahoot! and Duolingo to lifestyle companions such as Apple Fitness and Habitica. Perhaps you are already utilizing gamification in your classroom.

But can gamification really double your learning retention or increase your grade by one whole letter? Companies may try to convince you that their gamification product is a magic bullet, but in reality the best strategy depends on a variety of factors. Start your journey by reading about the different game elements, and then learn how to craft gamified lesson plans around your student needs through the scenario designer.
